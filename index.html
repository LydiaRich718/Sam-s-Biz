Q&A

Does insurance cover storm damage?

Tap to find out →

The Answer

Most homeowner policies cover wind and hail damage. If enough storm-related damage is found during inspection, your insurer is typically required to pay for a full replacement.

Will my rates go up?

Tap to find out →

The Answer

Storm damage claims are typically treated as "act of God" — different from at-fault claims. Rate impacts vary by carrier and state, but many homeowners see no increase at all.

How long does a replacement take?

Tap to find out →

The Answer

Most residential roofs are completed in a single day. Larger or more complex roofs may take two. We'll give you a clear timeline before we start.

What does it cost me?

Tap to find out →

The Answer

Most homeowners only pay their deductible — often $500–$1,500. Insurance covers the rest. We'll walk you through what to expect before anything is signed.

How do I know if I have damage?

Tap to find out →

The Answer

Missing shingles, granule loss, dents on vents, or water stains inside are common signs. Some damage is invisible from the ground — that's why we inspect for free.

What if my claim gets denied?

Tap to find out →

The Answer

We fight it. We're experienced with appeals and supplements — if damage was missed or underpaid, we go back to your insurer on your behalf at no extra cost.
